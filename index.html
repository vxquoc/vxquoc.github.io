About Me

Lover of physics, problem-solving, and Vietnamese coffee. I believe that science should serve both curiosity and community.

LinkedIn | Twitter | Google Scholar

My research lies at the intersection of soft matter physics and biomedical engineering, with a focus on understanding and controlling interfacial phenomena in soft, dynamic systems and applying these principles to develop biomedical devices. From elucidating the physical principles that govern droplet-substrate interactions to engineering organs-on-chips that mimic complex tissue microenvironments, my research advances both fundamental science and practical tools for translational medicine. By applying physical rigor to biological systems, I aim to develop novel platforms for disease modelling, drug testing, and personalized medicine

Research

My research combines experimental techniques and theoretical modeling to understand complex fluid dynamics, wetting phenomena, and their implications in bioengineering systems such as organ-on-chip platforms.
