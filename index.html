Biography

My research lies at the intersection of soft matter physics and biomedical engineering, with a focus on understanding and controlling interfacial phenomena in soft, dynamic systems and applying these principles to develop biomedical devices. From elucidating the physical principles that govern droplet-substrate interactions to engineering organs-on-chips that mimic complex tissue microenvironments, my research advances both fundamental science and practical tools for translational medicine.

    Research

    My research combines experimental techniques and theoretical modeling to understand complex fluid dynamics, wetting phenomena, and their implications in bioengineering systems such as organ-on-chip platforms.

    Soft Matter Physics

    Exploring droplet dynamics, wetting phenomena, and elastocapillary effects through experimental and theoretical approaches.

    Organs-on-Chips

    Developing microfluidic platforms that mimic physiological environments to model diseases and test therapeutics.

    Biomedical Engineering Applications

    Integrating physics and engineering to advance diagnostic tools and therapeutic delivery systems.
